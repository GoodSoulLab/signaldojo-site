Voice & Tone

Core voice attributes, tone spectrum, language rules, and what the voice sounds like and does not sound like. Built from Phase 6 of the Personal Brand Strategy and the Writing Style Prompt.

Core Voice

Grounded. Direct. Philosophically fluent.

Says the real thing with no setup, no softening, no filler. The reader always knows where they stand. Ideas arrive through lived experience and analogy, not lecture. Concepts earn their place by doing something useful. Peer-to-peer — not above the reader. Further along, not higher up. Precise over voluminous: one sharp insight beats five adequate ones.

Source: Phase 6 — Voice and Tone
Tone Spectrum

Warm but not soft. Intense in the best sense.

Cares about the work and it shows. Honest before polished. Does not need to be liked. Tends to be likable anyway. Sounds like a founder pitching a vision — even when the vision is for someone else. Someone who explains complex ideas through analogy the way a master teaches without naming the lesson.

Source: Phase 6 — Voice and Tone
Language Rules

Specific over general. Active over passive.

Avoid lengthy sentences. Right-size for the idea. Active voice always. One idea lands before the next one arrives. Specific over general: analogies and personal stories over abstract explanation. Lived experience first, frameworks second. No em dashes. No filler. No setup language.

Source: Phase 6 — Language Guidelines / Writing Style Prompt
Does Not Sound Like

Not a motivational speaker. Not a marketing copywriter.

Not someone performing passion rather than living it. Not clinical or textbook register when introducing psychological concepts. Not summarizing what the preceding paragraph just showed. Not over-structuring with numbered steps when prose would carry the instruction more naturally.

Source: Phase 6 — Voice and Tone / Writing Style Prompt
