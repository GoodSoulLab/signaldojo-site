01 — Foundation

Brand Position

The filter for every design decision. When in doubt, return here.

What this brand is

A precision training environment for people who have done the inner work and are ready for the outer architecture. Warm enough to make you feel found. Rigorous enough to make the work real.

What this brand is not

A productivity system. A motivational platform. A place that treats you as a conversion metric or a persona to be optimized.

What it feels like

Walking into a space built for exactly this kind of work. Quiet. Capable. Alive. The kind of stillness that is not empty but loaded. You feel seen before anything is asked of you.

The emotional contract

Safe first. Then heard. Then capable. Confidence is the outcome, not the entry point. This sequence is non-negotiable across every touchpoint.

Inner ↔ inhabits ↔ Outer · Soul ↔ inhabits ↔ Signal · Stillness ↔ inhabits ↔ Precision

The brand does not resolve this tension. It inhabits it. Design that flattens one side at the expense of the other is out of brand.

04 — Registers

The Two Registers

The brand's visual language shifts as the content shifts. The warm register carries inner foundation content. The cool register carries system and mechanism content. Both use the same type. The surface, the divider color, and the eyebrow treatment signal which register you are in.

Warm Register — Inner Foundation

Where the soul content lives.

Surface: Parchment (#F7F5F0). Dividers: rule-warm. Eyebrow: Ink Light. Used for: identity, rupture and return, self-trust, the gap, the return story.

Cool Register — Signal / System

Where the signal content lives.

Surface: Indigo Tint (#EEEEF8). Dividers: rule-cool. Eyebrow: Indigo Mid. Used for: the mechanism, prompt structure, the three levers, how it works.

Dark Register — High Contrast

Used for hero, final CTA, and navigation.

Surface: Ink (#1A1A1A). Text: White. Accent: Indigo Mid or Sand. Use sparingly. High-contrast moments only. The brand cannot live entirely in the dark — it must return to warmth.

06 — Components

Buttons

Three button types. One hierarchy. Never use more than two button types in the same visual zone. The primary action is always clear.

TypeUseRules
Primary (Filled Indigo) The one action that matters most per section. Purchase, enroll, join. One per visible viewport. Never compete with another primary.
Secondary (Outline Indigo) Mid-page CTAs. Secondary actions. Learn more, see how it works. Use when a primary CTA already exists nearby.
Ghost (Outline Neutral) Tertiary actions. Back, cancel, dismiss. Never use as a CTA. For navigation and destructive actions only.
Dark (Filled Ink) CTAs within dark sections where indigo would not contrast sufficiently. Use on dark surfaces only. Never on warm or cool surfaces.

All buttons use pill border-radius (999px). Font-size 0.875rem. Font-weight 700. Letter-spacing 0.04em. Transition: opacity 180ms ease, transform 120ms ease. Hover: opacity 0.85, translateY(-1px).

09 — Principles

Voice & Copy Rules

The brand speaks peer-to-peer. Not above. Not below. Further along, not higher up. Every word earns its place.

Write this way
  • Short sentences. One idea lands before the next arrives.
  • Active voice. Always.
  • Specific over general. Concrete image over abstract statement.
  • Lead with the felt experience. Framework arrives second.
  • Name the thing precisely. Do not soften it.
  • Trust the reader. Do not hand-hold.
  • Peer-to-peer. You are further along, not higher up.
Never write this way
  • Setup language. "In conclusion," "In summary," "Let me explain."
  • Motivational speaker register. Hype. Overclaiming.
  • Passive constructions. "Is designed to help you."
  • Em dashes. Use commas, periods, or semicolons.
  • Filler affirmations. "Great question." "Absolutely."
  • Abstract without grounding. Philosophy without lived texture.
  • Urgency manufacturing. Countdown timers, artificial scarcity.
Exact Language — Preserve These Precisely

"In the dark, I am the light that chooses love."

"I name it. I own it. I return to who I am."

"Where Science Meets Soul."

These phrases are load-bearing. Never paraphrase, reword, or split them. They arrive whole or not at all.

10 — Principles

Motion Principles

Motion is used with the same intentionality as space. The brand does not perform animation. It arrives with quiet confidence.

Transitions

Opacity and transform only. Never layout-affecting properties.

180ms ease

Hover States

Subtle lift. Never dramatic. Confirms interactivity without announcing it.

opacity 0.85, translateY(-1px)

Page Load

One orchestrated reveal with staggered delays. Not scattered micro-animations.

fadeIn + translateY(12px) → 0

Scroll Reveals

Content enters as the reader arrives at it. Reinforces the sense that the space responds to presence.

IntersectionObserver, 240ms ease-out

Duration Rule

Nothing moves for longer than 300ms. The brand does not linger. It arrives and settles.

100ms–300ms range

What Never Moves

Text. Layouts. Color fills on scroll. If it carries meaning, it does not animate.

prefers-reduced-motion: respect it always

12 — Usage Rules

What This System Protects

These rules exist because the brand position document has specific requirements. Violating them does not produce a different aesthetic — it produces a different brand.

  • ◆ The emotional contract is safe first, then capable. Never lead with aspiration, confidence, or transformation language before the person feels recognized. Problem before solution. Every time.
  • ◆ The two registers must coexist. A page built entirely in the cool register loses the soul layer. A page built entirely in the warm register loses the signal layer. Both must be present on every major touchpoint.
  • ◆ Fraunces italic is used once per headline maximum. It signals: this is the phrase that matters. Use it more than once and it signals nothing.
  • ◆ The dark surface is used for arrival and departure — hero and final CTA — and nowhere else. The brand cannot live in the dark. It must return to warmth.
  • ◆ The primary CTA is one per section. Never two filled buttons competing. The decision is always clear.
  • ◆ Motion is quiet. Nothing performs. The brand arrives with confidence, not announcement. If an animation calls attention to itself, remove it.
  • ◆ Exact language phrases are load-bearing. They are never paraphrased, split, or italicized for stylistic effect. They arrive whole.
  • ◆ The brand must never feel like it is processing a person. If a design decision makes the user feel like one of many, it is out of brand regardless of how aesthetically consistent it is.
