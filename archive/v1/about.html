About

I have been studying
one problem for
14 years.

The gap between who you are capable of being and who you are actually being.

For a decade I built products in digital health and mental healthcare designed to change behavior at scale. I learned something most systems miss: the gap is not a knowledge problem. People are not missing information. They are missing a working system for converting what they know into how they consistently show up.

I kept solving it for companies. The gap kept showing up in the people building them.

Then it showed up in me.

I went through a long pattern of rupture and return. Each time I came back with more awareness. Each time the coping mechanisms that had carried me got more brittle. The last episode was the longest. It forced the deepest work. What I found underneath was a root I had not fully named: performance-based self-worth and the things I had made unspeakable.

That work changed everything I thought I knew about sustainable performance.

Self-trust is not a feeling you wait for. It is built through action.

Identity shifts when the body is included, not just the mind. Sustainable contribution is built from the inside out, identity first, then strategy, then output.

I pursued the Heroic coaching certification and workshop instructor program not to add letters after my name but because I needed the framework and the community. I know this world from the inside.

I built Signal Dojo because I needed it myself. The same operating system I used to build and ship products at startups and health companies, applied to the one product most coaches, consultants, and founders never build systematically: themselves.

Ship. Measure. Learn. Decide. Repeat.
